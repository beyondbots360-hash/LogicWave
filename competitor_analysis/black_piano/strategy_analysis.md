# Strategy Analysis: Black Piano

## Overview
Black Piano is a direct competitor to Logic Wave, focusing on bridging the gap between UK businesses and Indian talent. They position themselves as a "British owned" company, which is a major trust-building USP for UK-based clients.

## Key Value Propositions
- **British Owned & Managed**: They emphasize UK leadership to mitigate cultural and legal concerns for clients.
- **Full-Lifecycle Management**: They don't just recruit; they provide Employer of Record (EOR) and Remote HR services, ensuring compliance with local laws (India) and global standards.
- **Transparency**: Their pricing model explicitly separates the "Employee Cost" from the "Management Fee," which builds trust through financial transparency.
- **High-Touch Sales**: The website heavily promotes direct scheduling with the founder (Jonathan), suggesting a personalized, consultative sales approach.

## Market Positioning
- **Trust-First**: Uses Case Studies and "British-owned" tags to differentiate from pure Indian outsourcing firms.
- **Educator Persona**: The "Journal" section contains deep-dive guides (IR35, EOR vs PEO, Payroll trends), positioning them as subject matter experts in international employment.

## Comparison with Logic Wave
- **Similarities**: UK-India focus, wide range of roles (Tech, Admin, Creative), EOR focus.
- **Logic Wave Opportunity**: Logic Wave can differentiate by highlighting its specific AI integration (Writing Studio, SEO tools) and perhaps a more "modern/tech-forward" interface compared to what seems like a more traditional "agency" feel of Black Piano.
